Our Game Model

We play proactive, attacking football. We build from the back, press high out of possession, and value intelligence and bravery on the ball. Our system relies on technical execution and constant communication.

  • In Possession: Expand the field. Look to break lines. Be brave in 1v1 situations in the final third.
  • Out of Possession: Immediate counter-press upon losing the ball. Stay compact and deny central penetration.
  • Transition: React instantly. Find the highest open player when winning the ball. Sprint to recovery positions when losing it.
Tactical Setup
Positions & Roles
Select a position on the field to view specific responsibilities.
Goalkeeper #1

In Possession: Initiate attacks from saves and crosses. Be an option for back passes.

Out of Possession: Stop shots. Cover space behind defenders. Organize defensive line constantly.

Full Backs #2 & #3

In Possession: Provide width and depth. Make overlapping runs.

Out of Possession: Stop crosses. Provide compactness. Track overlapping runners.

Center Backs #4 & #5

In Possession: Split to edges of box. Break lines with passes.

Out of Possession: Command the defensive line. Win aerial duels. Stay compact.

Midfielders #6, #8, #10

In Possession: Control the tempo. Connect defense to attack. Provide passing angles.

Out of Possession: Screen the backline. Counter-press immediately. Deny central passes.

Wingers #7 & #11

In Possession: Stay high and wide. Take on 1v1s. Cross into the box.

Out of Possession: Press the opponent's fullbacks. Track back to defend the wide areas.

Center Forward #9

In Possession: Stretch the defense with runs in behind. Hold up the ball.

Out of Possession: Initiate the press. Force play one direction. Block passes to holding mid.

Team Language
Communication
Key phrases used on the field to ensure we play as one unit.
Space!
Open area available — move into it or pass to it.
Drop!
Come back and offer a passing option or retreat defensively.
Switch!
Change the point of attack to the opposite flank.
Keeper's!
GK calling for the ball — clear out immediately.
Overlap!
Run beyond the ball carrier to create an extra option.
Through!
Split the defense with a pass in behind the line.
One-Two!
Quick give-and-go pass and movement.
Shot!
Encouraging a teammate in a good position to shoot.
