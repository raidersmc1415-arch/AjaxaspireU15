Our Game Model

We play proactive, attacking football. With build up play, we'll go around-through-over, use quick combination play to advance the ball. We value intelligence and bravery on the ball. Defensively everyone is a defender mentality, smart but physical when defending. Our system relies on high effort, high attitude, technical execution and constant communication.

Tactical Setup
Positions & Roles
Select a position on the field to view specific expectations.
Goalkeeper #1

In Possession: Initiate attacks from saves and crosses. Be an option for back passes.

Out of Possession: Stop shots. Cover space behind defenders. Organize defensive line constantly.

Right Back #2

In Possession: Provide width and depth on the right flank. Make overlapping/underlapping runs.

Out of Possession: Stop crosses. Provide compactness. Track runners and shift with the back line.

Left Back #3

In Possession: Provide width and depth on the left flank. Make overlapping/underlapping runs.

Out of Possession: Stop crosses. Provide compactness. Track runners and shift with the back line.

Center Backs #4 & #5

In Possession: Split to edges of box. Composure on the ball in the build. Break lines with passes or idenfity when to carry the ball forward.

Out of Possession: Command the defensive line. Win aerial duels. Stay compact. Recognize when theres space in behind an delay first principle, make good defensive decisions.

Holding Midfielder #6

In Possession: Anchor the build-up by receiving with an open body to break lines, connecting our back four to the #8/#10, and serving as the primary outlet to switch play in the attacking half.

Out of Possession: Protect the back 4 by maintaing position discipline and connection no more than 10 yards away. Intercept passes into the opponents 9. Compete to win second balls in the air from opponet keeper or long balls. Work hard to stay in-line or behind the ball in transition.

Central and Attacking Midfielders #8 & #10

In Possession: Operate in the half-spaces. Connect midfield to attack, break lines with passes or dribbles, and provide the primary creative spark in the final third. Counter-press immediately upon losing the ball. The 10 will operate higher on the pitch, dropping into the windows on build outs, looking to receive and play forward or quick combination play with wide players or central to the 9. The 8 is the engine, box to box midfielder who will organize the attack, change the pace (slow the attack down or quick counter), high work rate.br>
Out of Possession: The 8 will work to track back immediately to support the 6, same principle work hard to get in line or behind the ball in transition. The 10 will need to track back immediately if the ball is lost on their strong side, weak side identify opponents making the counter attack runs and mark them in transition. The 10 almost always marks the opponent 6 in the build out. In a mid block, the 10 will support the 9 to start the press when triggers are identified.

Wingers #7 & #11

In Possession: Create width when the ball is strong side by putting heals on the touch line. Recognize when to cut in with the ball. Take on 1v1s, space in behind, final 3rd. Cross into the box. Rest defense, weakside should be tracking the play and tucking into central channels and be ready for a turnover and stop the counter.

Out of Possession: Press the opponent's fullbacks. Track back to defend the wide areas. Lose the ball, fight to win it back.

Center Forward #9

In Possession: Stretch the defense with runs in behind. Create depth by pinning the opponent back 4 high up the pitch. Hold up the ball when you can't immediately turn, look to combine with your 8/10 when facing your own goal. Arrive at the penalty spot for crosses into the box.

Out of Possession: Initiate the press. Force play one direction. Block passes to holding mid. When in a block, look for pressing triggers to start the press and cut off switch of play.
