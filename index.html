This is our identity. We don't play one way — we read the game and solve the problem in front of us. Our three build-out plans, our defensive spectrum, our attacking patterns — all of it flows from this principle. Smart football. Fluid football.

Our Game Model

We play proactive, intelligent football. In possession we build with purpose — going around, through, or over the opponent's pressure depending on what the game presents. We value technical quality, spatial awareness, and bravery on the ball. Out of possession, every player is a defender from the moment we lose it. Our system demands high effort, high standards, and constant communication — but above all, the ability to read the game and make the right decision.

✅ In Possession
1 — Look Forward First
Look forward first. If it's on, play it. If not, keep the ball and reset — patience creates the moment, bravery takes it.
2 — Create Superiority Off the Ball
Move to create an advantage — never stand in the same line as a teammate. Every run either opens space for you or opens space for someone else.
3 — Recognize the Moment and Commit
When space opens behind their line — take it. Be brave in 1v1s. Hesitation is the only mistake.
4 — Build Until the Moment Arrives. Then Be Ruthless.
Quality over volume. We don't force chances — we earn them. When the opportunity comes, we finish it.
🛡️ Out of Possession
1 — Defending Starts at the Front
Every player is a defender the moment we lose the ball. This is a mentality, not a position.
2 — Five Seconds. Hunt.
The moment we lose the ball is the moment we win it back. The opponent is most vulnerable in the first five seconds after receiving. Counter-press immediately.
3 — Defend as One Body
Get compact. Shrink the field. No daylight between the lines. We don't defend as individuals — we defend as a connected unit.
4 — Know Your Role in the Moment
Every player out of possession is always one of three things: the pressure, the cover, or the balance. Read the game. Know which one you are right now.
Our Defensive Spectrum
⬆️ High Press
Coordinated, trigger-based. We press as a unit when the cue fires — bad first touch, slow pass, opponent facing their own goal, GK with ball. Half-pressing is worse than not pressing. Commit fully or hold your shape.
➡️ Mid Block (4-4-2)
Zonal compactness. Two banks connected and staggered — we hold our shape and force play into predictable corridors. Don't chase runners out of your zone. Move as a unit. Squeeze when the trigger fires on the touchline.
⬇️ Emergency / Box Defense
When we're defending our 18, zonal is over. Grab your mark. Emergency defending — every player accounts for a body. Clear your lines. Live to play another day. No heroics — defend first.
Player Standards
What We Do
  • Hold yourself and your teammates to a high standard — with kindness
  • Bring energy to every session. High fives, encouragement, focus
  • Be coachable. Listen, ask questions, apply the feedback
  • Show up on time, ready — cleats on, phone away
  • Support teammates on and off the field. No one stands alone
  • Take ownership of your development. The work you do away from training defines you
What We Don't Do
  • Gossip, negativity, or poor conduct on social media — it reflects on all of us
  • Direct frustration at teammates. Compete with each other, not against each other
  • Confuse being loud with being a leader. Leadership is demonstrated, not declared
  • Use poor body language. How you carry yourself sends a message to your team
  • Make excuses or point fingers. Take accountability and move forward
  • Be casual about punctuality. Being late communicates that your time matters more than everyone else's

Tactical Setup
Positions & Roles
Select a position on the field to view specific expectations.
Goalkeeper · #1

In Possession: You start every attack. Read the press before the ball arrives and make the decision — distribute with purpose, not panic. Be a composed outlet for back passes. Use your footwork as a weapon. Initiate attacks quickly from saves and crosses with accurate distribution to the right target.

Out of Possession: Organize the defensive line constantly — your voice sets the shape. Cover the space behind defenders as the team pushes up. Command your box on aerial balls. Call off the back four clearly and early. Your composure and communication under pressure sets the tone for the whole team.

Right Back · #2

In Possession: Provide width and depth on the right flank. Read when the winger cuts inside — that's your trigger to overlap into the space they've vacated. Tuck into the half-space when the ball is on the opposite side. Connect with the 6 and 8 as a third-man option in the build. Drive forward when the channel is open.

Out of Possession: Stop crosses at the source. Stay compact and connected to the center backs. When defending your wide channel with an overlapping runner, track the runner — the center backs shift to cover the ball carrier. In the mid block, hold your zone. In emergency box defense, grab your mark and defend it.

Left Back · #3

In Possession: Provide width and depth on the left flank. Read when the winger cuts inside — that's your trigger to overlap into the vacated wide channel. Tuck into the half-space when the ball is on the opposite side. Be a reliable outlet in the build and a threat in the final third when the lane opens.

Out of Possession: Stop crosses at the source. Stay compact and connected to the center backs. When defending your wide channel with an overlapping runner, track the runner — the center backs shift to cover the ball carrier. In the mid block, hold your zone. In emergency box defense, grab your mark and defend it.

Center Backs · #4 & #5

In Possession: Split wide to the edges of the box to receive and draw the press. Composure on the ball is non-negotiable — your calm starts the attack. Look to break lines with passes into the midfield windows. Scan before the ball arrives. Know your next pass before you receive it. Join the attack when the channel opens — you have permission to carry forward.

Out of Possession: Command the defensive line — communicate constantly. Delay first when space is behind you — do not dive in. Direct the opponent away from goal, then find the moment to engage. Win aerial duels and clear with authority. In the mid block, move as a connected unit. In emergency box defense, grab your mark, defend your space, and clear your lines first.

Holding Midfielder · #6

In Possession: You are the connection between the back four and the attack. Receive with an open body — scan before the ball arrives and know where your next pass is going. Serve as the primary relief valve when the fullbacks are pressed. Look to break lines into the 8 and 10's half-space pockets. In build-out you are the pivot — Plan B lives and dies with your ability to receive, turn, and play forward immediately. Be the third-man option when the ball goes wide.

Out of Possession: Protect the back four. Maintain positional discipline — stay connected, never more than 10-12 yards from the defensive line. Intercept passes aimed at the opponent's striker. Compete for second balls. Work hard to stay in line or behind the ball in every transition — if you're caught high, you've exposed the back four.

Central & Attacking Midfielders · #8 & #10

In Possession — #10: Operate higher between the opponent's lines — in the window behind their press. Receive on the half-turn, already scanning, already knowing where you're going. Your first thought is always forward. In build-out you drop into the pocket space to connect — receive, turn, and penetrate. Combine quickly with wide players and the 9. You are the creative spark.

In Possession — #8: You are the engine. Box to box. Receive from the 6 and drive the attack forward. Read when to change the pace — when to slow it down and when to go. Your energy and work rate sets the standard for the entire team. Make yourself available constantly.

Out of Possession — #8: Track back immediately to support the 6. Get in line or behind the ball in transition — no exceptions. Your defensive work rate enables the team's pressing shape.

Out of Possession — #10: If the ball is lost on your strong side, track back immediately. On the weak side, identify runners making counter-attack runs and cut them off in transition. In the mid block, work with the 9 to screen the opponent's pivot and trigger the press when the cue fires.

Wingers · #7 & #11

In Possession: When the ball is on your side, heels on the touchline — your width is what stretches their back four and creates half-space pockets for the 8 and 10. Read when to cut inside and when to hold width. Take on 1v1s in the final third — be brave, be direct. Deliver crosses on the ground to the front post. Weak-side winger: track the play, tuck into the central channel, and be ready for a turnover or a switch of play arriving at speed.

Out of Possession: Press the opponent's fullbacks immediately. Your press angle should cut off the pass back to their center backs. Track back to support your fullback in wide defensive situations. Lose the ball — you're the first to chase it. In the mid block, hold your horizontal line and don't get pulled out of shape by runners.

Center Forward · #9

In Possession: Your job starts before the ball arrives — pin the opponent's back line deep with smart movement. Keep them honest. Don't stand next to defenders; make them find you. When you receive facing your own goal, hold the ball and combine with the 8 or 10 arriving in support. Make runs in behind to exploit space. Arrive at the penalty spot for crosses. Follow every shot — the rebound is yours if you're there.

Out of Possession: You start the press. Your angle of approach sets the direction the whole team defends. Approach on a curve to cut off the pass back to the goalkeeper — force play one side. Block the opponent's pivot. In the mid block, be patient — wait for the pressing trigger, then commit fully. In the high press, you are the spark that ignites the team.
