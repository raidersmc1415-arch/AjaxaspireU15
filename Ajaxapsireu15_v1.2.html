Team Principles
Our Game Model
How we play in every moment of the game — learn it, own it
✅ In Possession
1
Look Forward, Play Forward, Keep the BallDon't force the situation. Read what's in front of you before acting.
2
Off the Ball MovementSupport the player on the ball. Create chances. Join the attack with speed.
3
Know When to go 1v1 — Be BraveIf there's space in behind — take it. Don't hesitate.
4
Create High Quality ChancesNot just shots — quality shots on goal from dangerous positions.
🛡️ Out of Possession
1
All Players are DefendersThis is a mindset. Every player defends from the front.
2
Immediately Counter PressWhen we lose the ball — Hunt. Don't wait.
3
Get Compact — BlockCompact in the middle and defending thirds. No space through the center.
4
Pressure – Cover – BalanceDelay · Direct · Deny. Read the game. Be disciplined.

🟩 By Third of the Field
Defending Third

In Possession
If a forward first option isn't available, secure the ball with quick passing, allow time for team shape to expand and form.

Out of Possession
Man marking. No free runs to goal. Defend in numbers. Keeper provides direction. Clear in doubt.
Middle Third

In Possession
Combination play. Off-ball movement. Wide players provide width. #9 provides height. CBs give depth.

Out of Possession
Delay–Direct–Deny. Get compact, shut central channel. Pressing cues: bad touch, facing own goal.
Attacking Third

In Possession
Penetrate the box, be brave when 1 v 1 opportunities exist, use overlaps, underlaps, quick give and gos to create overloads and move the defense to create quality shots on goal.

Out of Possession
#9 starts press from goal kicks. 2v1 on the sideline with 7 or 11. Recovery run if they escape.

Positional Guide
Roles & Responsibilities
Click your number on the field to see your job on both sides of the ball
Goalkeeper #1
In Possession
Distribute effectively — over, around, or through. Be an option for back passes. Initiate attacks from saves and crosses.
Clear communication to outfielders. Call off the back 4 when securing the ball.
Out of Possession
Cover space behind defenders as team pushes up. Organize defensive line constantly.
Vocal on pressing/marking. Stop and save shots. Provide direction to the whole team.
Full Backs #2 & #3
In Possession
Provide width and depth. Make overlapping runs when wide players come inside. Drive into the central channel to attract opponents and unlock midfielders.
Out of Possession
Stop crosses. Provide compactness. When defending a wide channel with an overlapping runner — go with the runner. #4 & #5 shift to cover the ball carrier.
Center Backs #4 & #5
In Possession
Provide depth at diagonal 5–7 yards. Play line-breaking passes into midfield. Join attack when space is open. Heavy communication — you see the whole picture.
Out of Possession
DELAY first — give team time to track back. Stay compact through center. Know how many #9s are in play. Delay–Direct–Deny on counter attacks. Don't go for tackles unless you're certain.
Defensive Midfielders #6 & #8
In Possession
Connection to back 4. Receive in space with open body — first check #7/#11. Be the relief valve at the bottom of the triangle between #8 and #10.
Out of Possession
First to challenge punts from keeper. Break up counter attacks. Win second balls. Create 2v1 defending situations in opposition build-out.
Attacking Midfielder #10
In Possession
#10 has freedom — first pass should break the last defensive line. #8 is more disciplined — creative on strong side, supportive on weak side. #6 holds the bottom of the triangle.
Out of Possession
Even or behind the ball in transition. Immediate counter press. #10 joins #9 in 4-4-2 block and signals when to press based on triggers: bad touch, facing own goal, slow pass.
Wide Players #7 & #11
In Possession
Stay high and wide to provide width. Vary your runs — straight, diagonal, L-shape. Cross low to front post from wide channels. Take on 1v1s — be brave. Weak-side player must arrive in the box on time for crosses.
Out of Possession
Strong-side 7/11 must be even with the ball. Don't allow switch of play. Recovery run when ball is lost. Support #9 in a 2v1 on the sideline to win the ball back.
Centre Forward #9
In Possession
Stay high to create height and keep defenders out of build play. Don't stand next to defenders — make them find you. Drop to create a midfield diamond when needed.
Out of Possession
Start the press from goal kicks. 2v1 with #7 or #11 on the sideline to win the ball. Prevent switch of play. Read the game and communicate.

Learn these phrases. Use them every session. Click any card for the full detail.

Opponent right behind you — act fast
Time!
No pressure — take a moment, look up
Turn!
Space behind you — safe to turn
Space!
Open area available — move into it
Drop!
Come back and offer a passing option
Switch!
Change the point of attack now
Keeper's!
GK calling for the ball — clear out
Overlap!
Run beyond to create an extra option
Through!
Split the defense with a pass in behind
One-Two!
Give-and-go — pass and move now
Shot!
You're in position — take it!
Back Door!
Far post run — space behind defenders

Google Sites Setup Guide
Step-by-step instructions to build this hub in Google Sites
🔐 Access Control Strategy
Public Pages (anyone with link):
Game Model · Positions · Communication · Schedule

Player-Only Pages (Google Account required):
IDP Pages · Film Room · Reflection responses

How to restrict pages:
In Google Sites → Page Settings → Restrict access → add each player's Gmail address individually
🛠️ Tools to Embed
Google FormsSession + game reflection forms — Insert → Embed
Google Drive FolderVideo uploads — share with team as Editor, embed via Insert → Drive
Google SheetsIDP tracker per player — embed on their private page
VeoRequired for GA — link from Film Room page
📋 Build Order — 6 Steps
01

Create Your Google Site

Go to sites.google.com → New Site. Name it AJAX Aspire U15 – Team Hub. Set sharing to Anyone with the link can view for public pages.

02

Build the Page Structure

Add pages: Home · Game Model · Positions · Communication · Player Portal · Reflections · Film Room · Parent Hub.

03

Create Private IDP Pages per Player

Under Player Portal, add a sub-page per player. In Page Settings → Restrict access, add only that player's Gmail + your coach email.

04

Set Up the Reflection Google Form

Create a Google Form with all reflection fields. Use Insert → Embed to add it to the Reflections page. All responses auto-save to a linked Google Sheet.

05

Film Room — Google Drive + Veo

Create a shared Drive folder: AJAX Aspire U15 – Film Room 25/26. Share with team as Editor. Embed via Insert → Drive. Add a Veo link for GA compliance.

06

Publish and Share

Click Publish → set URL to sites.google.com/view/ajaxaspireu15. Share the main link publicly. Send individual IDP links directly to each player.

⚠️ Google Sites requires players to be signed into a Google account for page-level restrictions to work. For strongest IDP privacy, consider using Google Classroom as a companion tool.
